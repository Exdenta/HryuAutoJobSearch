# Privacy Policy

**Last updated: 24 April 2026**

This policy describes what the Job Alert Bot (the "Bot") collects when you
use it on Telegram, how that data is used, who else sees it, and how you can
delete it.

The Bot is operated by Lex Sherman, an individual, not a company. If anything
in this policy is unclear or you want to exercise a right described below,
email **lexsherman.official@gmail.com**.

## What we collect

When you message the Bot, we collect and store the following on the server
that runs the Bot:

1. **Telegram identifiers** — your `chat_id`, `username`, `first_name`, and
   `last_name` as provided by the Telegram Bot API. Required to send you
   messages and tell your data apart from other users'.
2. **Your résumé**, if you upload one. The PDF file is saved on the server;
   its text is extracted and stored in a local database so we can match
   postings against your experience and generate per-role analyses.
3. **Your preferences**, if you send them via `/prefs` or the setup wizard
   (target role, seniority, location, remote policy, minimum match score,
   free-text description).
4. **A structured profile**, generated by passing your résumé and
   preferences to an AI model (see "Third-party services" below). Kept on
   the server to filter and rank postings for you.
5. **Per-role interactions** — which postings you marked as applied or not
   a fit, which you requested tailored résumés for, and which you ran a
   fit analysis on. Kept so the same posting isn't shown twice and so we
   can restore a cached analysis when you re-tap a button.
6. **Operational metadata** — timestamps of when the profile was built,
   audit rows recording AI calls (duration, model, success or failure),
   and message IDs so button presses can be linked back to the job they
   came from.

We do **not** collect your phone number, location, browsing history outside
the Bot, contacts, or any other information you don't send us.

## How we use it

- To send you a daily digest of job postings matched to your profile.
- To score each posting 0-5 against your résumé using an AI model.
- To generate, on your explicit request, a tailored résumé draft or a
  "fit analysis" (strengths, gaps, recommendation) for a specific posting.
- To produce, on your explicit request, a deep market-research report for
  your role and location.
- To remember which postings you've already seen or acted on, so we don't
  repeat them.

We never use your data for advertising, sell it, or share it with people
outside the services listed below.

## Third-party services

Using the Bot means your data passes through these third parties:

- **Telegram** — every message you send to or receive from the Bot
  transits Telegram's servers. See [Telegram's privacy policy](https://telegram.org/privacy).
- **Anthropic (Claude)** — when you upload a résumé, set preferences, tap
  "Tailor resume", tap "Analyze fit", or run `/marketresearch`, the Bot
  sends the relevant excerpts of your résumé, your preferences, and the
  job posting to Anthropic's Claude API. See [Anthropic's privacy policy](https://www.anthropic.com/privacy).
  Your résumé is never uploaded to Anthropic *unless* you trigger one of
  those features.
- **Job-board websites** — LinkedIn, Indeed, Hacker News "Who is Hiring",
  and a set of curated remote-job boards. The Bot retrieves postings from
  their public search endpoints; it does not send any information about
  you to them.
- **Web search** (for `/marketresearch`) — market-research sub-agents
  perform web searches that include your target location and role keywords
  (not your name, résumé, or Telegram identifiers) to gather salary and
  demand information.

## How long we keep it

- **Your résumé, profile, preferences, and interaction history** — kept
  until you delete them. There is no automatic expiration.
- **Cached AI outputs** (profile, tailored résumés, fit analyses, market
  research reports) — kept until the input changes or you delete them.
- **Audit logs** of AI calls — kept indefinitely for debugging and
  reliability, but contain no résumé text: only hashes, timestamps, status,
  and error headings truncated to 200 characters.

## Your rights

You can delete any of your data at any time, with no questions asked.
Use `/cleardata` in the Bot and choose one of:

- **Resume** — deletes the PDF file, the extracted text, and every cached
  fit analysis that was run against it.
- **Job history** — deletes your applied/not-a-fit marks and the log of
  postings already shown to you.
- **Tailored resumes** — deletes every tailored-résumé draft the Bot has
  generated for you.
- **Profile** — deletes your free-text preferences, your structured profile,
  and the profile-build audit rows.
- **Research** — deletes every market-research report you've generated and
  their audit rows.
- **Everything** — deletes all of the above plus your user record. You will
  need to send `/start` and re-onboard if you want to use the Bot again.

Deletions are immediate and irreversible.

If you'd prefer to exercise a specific GDPR or similar right (access,
export, rectification, portability), email the address above. Because the
Bot is run by an individual, response time is best-effort and usually
within a few days.

## Security

- The server running the Bot is access-controlled and not publicly shared.
- The database and résumé files are stored on the server's local filesystem;
  they are not uploaded to any cloud service.
- The Bot's Telegram token is stored in a local `.env` file and is not
  published.
- Data sent to Anthropic is transmitted over TLS. Anthropic's handling of
  that data is governed by the policy linked above.

No service is perfectly secure. If you consider your résumé sensitive, use
`/cleardata` after you've received the outputs you wanted.

## Children

The Bot is not intended for users under the age of 16. If you believe a
child has sent résumé data to the Bot, email the address above and we will
delete it.

## Changes to this policy

If we change what data we collect or how it's used, the "Last updated"
date above will change and the Bot will post a one-line notice in your
chat pointing to the new version. You can also run `/privacy` in the Bot
at any time to see the current version.
